United Nations Security Council

The UNSC will simulate the tense days of the Cuban Missile Crisis, frozen on 14 October 1962, when the United States discovered Soviet nuclear missiles stationed in Cuba. Delegates will step into the shoes of world leaders navigating one of the most dangerous confrontations of the Cold War. The committee will focus on diplomacy, negotiation, and crisis management, as nations debate military action, sanctions, and peaceful resolution to prevent nuclear escalation. This simulation challenges participants to balance national interests with global security, reflecting the UNSC’s role as the guardian of international peace.

×

United Nations Human Rights Council

The UNHRC will deliberate on the Rwandan Genocide of 1994, one of the most tragic failures of the international community to protect human rights. Delegates will examine the causes, consequences, and humanitarian response to the mass atrocities that claimed hundreds of thousands of lives. The committee will emphasize accountability, justice, and the protection of vulnerable populations, mirroring the UNHRC’s mandate to uphold and promote human rights worldwide. Participants will be tasked with drafting resolutions that address humanitarian aid, refugee protection, and mechanisms to prevent future genocides.

International Press Corps

The IPC will assign delegates to cover proceedings in both the UNSC and UNHRC. Acting as journalists, correspondents, and media representatives, IPC members will report on debates, highlight key developments, and shape public perception of the committees’ actions. This body ensures transparency and adds a dynamic layer to the conference, as press coverage influences diplomatic decision-making. Delegates in the IPC will practice investigative reporting, editorial writing, and real-time news dissemination, capturing the essence of international media in global affairs.
