About School MUN

Our Conference

School MUN (Model United Nations) is an academic simulation of the United Nations where students assume the roles of ambassadors and participate in simulated UN committees. Our conference aims to educate participants about civics, effective communication, globalization, and multilateral diplomacy.

About School MUN

The School Model United Nations (MUN) program has been cultivating young leaders since 2010, providing students with a platform to develop their diplomatic, public speaking, and critical thinking skills.

Key Features of Our Conference:

  • Six diverse committees covering security, health, judicial, scientific, and historical topics
  • Experienced faculty advisors and student leadership team
  • Training workshops for both beginners and advanced delegates
  • Crisis simulations that test delegates' quick thinking and adaptability
  • Networking opportunities with students from across the region
  • Awards recognizing outstanding delegates in various categories

Who Should Participate?

Our conference welcomes students from all levels of experience. Whether you're a first-time delegate or a seasoned MUN participant, our diverse committees ensure there's a perfect fit for your interests and skill level.

United Nations Security Council (UNSC)

Committee Level:

Advanced

Topics:

  1. The situation in the South China Sea
  2. Cybersecurity threats to international peace

Delegation Size:

1 delegate per country

Recommended For:

Delegates with prior MUN experience interested in global security issues

Committee Description:

The United Nations Security Council (UNSC) is one of the six principal organs of the United Nations, charged with ensuring international peace and security. This committee is known for its fast-paced nature and high-stakes debates on contemporary global security challenges. Delegates will need to demonstrate strong diplomatic skills, strategic thinking, and the ability to negotiate under pressure.

What to Expect:

  • Intensive debates on current international crises
  • Opportunity to draft and pass binding resolutions
  • Possible crisis scenarios requiring immediate response
  • Use of veto power by permanent members
  • High-level diplomatic interactions

United Nations General Assembly (UNGA)

Committee Level:

All Levels

Topics:

  1. Global access to education and digital literacy
  2. Climate change and sustainable development

Delegation Size:

1 delegate per country

Recommended For:

First-time MUN participants and those interested in broad international policy discussions

Committee Description:

The United Nations General Assembly (UNGA) is the main deliberative body of the UN, where all member states have equal representation. It addresses a wide range of global issues including development, human rights, and international cooperation. This committee encourages inclusive dialogue and consensus-building, making it ideal for delegates looking to explore diplomacy in a collaborative setting.

What to Expect:

  • Engaging discussions on global development and humanitarian issues
  • Drafting and voting on non-binding resolutions
  • Focus on consensus and multilateral cooperation
  • Opportunities to practice public speaking and negotiation
  • Inclusive and educational environment

World Health Organization (WHO)

Committee Level:

Intermediate

Topics:

  1. Global response to future pandemics
  2. Mental health as a global priority

Delegation Size:

1 delegate per country

Recommended For:

Delegates interested in public health, policy-making, and humanitarian issues

Committee Description:

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. This committee focuses on pressing health challenges and policy coordination among nations. Delegates will engage in evidence-based discussions, propose innovative solutions, and collaborate to improve global health outcomes.

What to Expect:

  • Policy debates on global health systems and emergency preparedness
  • Collaborative drafting of health-related resolutions
  • Focus on scientific data and humanitarian impact
  • Opportunities to simulate international cooperation in health crises
  • Inclusive and research-driven environment

International Court of Justice (ICJ)

Committee Level:

Advanced

Case Topics:

  1. Territorial dispute between two sovereign nations
  2. Violation of international humanitarian law

Delegation Size:

Individual roles (judges, advocates, registrars)

Recommended For:

Experienced delegates with strong analytical and legal reasoning skills

Committee Description:

The International Court of Justice (ICJ) is the principal judicial organ of the United Nations, tasked with settling legal disputes between states and providing advisory opinions on international legal questions. This committee simulates real-world legal proceedings, requiring delegates to interpret treaties, present arguments, and deliver judgments based on international law.

What to Expect:

  • Structured courtroom-style proceedings
  • Detailed legal research and case preparation
  • Presentation of oral arguments and written memorials
  • Judicial deliberation and verdict formulation
  • Immersive experience in international law and diplomacy

United Nations Commission on Science and Technology for Development (UNCSTD)

Committee Focus:

Tech-Focused

Topics:

  1. Bridging the global digital divide
  2. Ethical governance of emerging technologies

Delegation Size:

1 delegate per country

Recommended For:

Delegates passionate about technology, innovation, and sustainable development

Committee Description:

The UNCSTD is a functional commission of the UN Economic and Social Council, dedicated to harnessing science and technology for sustainable development. This committee explores how innovation can address global challenges, reduce inequality, and promote inclusive growth. Delegates will analyze policy frameworks, propose tech-driven solutions, and debate the ethical implications of emerging technologies.

What to Expect:

  • Discussions on digital infrastructure and access
  • Debates on AI, biotechnology, and data ethics
  • Collaborative drafting of development-oriented resolutions
  • Focus on innovation, equity, and sustainability
  • Opportunities to shape global tech policy

Historical Crisis Committee (HCC)

Committee Type:

Historical

Topics:

  1. The Cuban Missile Crisis
  2. The Partition of India

Delegation Size:

Individual character roles

Recommended For:

Experienced delegates who enjoy fast-paced, historically immersive simulations

Committee Description:

The Historical Crisis Committee (HCC) transports delegates to pivotal moments in history, challenging them to rewrite the course of events through diplomacy, strategy, and quick decision-making. With dynamic updates and evolving scenarios, this committee demands adaptability, historical knowledge, and creative problem-solving.

What to Expect:

  • Immersive roleplay in historical settings
  • Real-time crisis updates and rapid responses
  • Opportunities to alter historical outcomes
  • Collaborative and competitive diplomacy
  • Engaging character-driven debates
